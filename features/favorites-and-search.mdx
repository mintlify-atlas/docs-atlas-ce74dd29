## Favorites

The **Favorites** tab shows only the doors you have marked as favorites. It supports the same search and unlock functionality as the main **Doors** tab, so you can find and open your go-to doors without scrolling through the full list.

Favorites are saved locally on your device and persist across sessions — they are still there the next time you open the app.

<Tip>
  Add the doors you use every day — your office suite, the lobby, the main entrance — to Favorites so they are always one tap away.
</Tip>

### Adding a door to Favorites

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Doors tab">
    Tap **Doors** in the bottom navigation bar to open the full door list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the door">
    Scroll or use the search bar to locate the door you want to save.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the star icon">
    Each door card shows a star icon. Tap it to add the door to your favorites. The icon fills in to confirm the door has been saved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your Favorites tab">
    Switch to the **Favorites** tab to confirm the door now appears there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Removing a door from Favorites

Tap the filled star icon on any door card — in either the **Doors** tab or the **Favorites** tab — to remove it from your favorites. The change takes effect immediately.

### How favorites are stored

Favorites are saved using the device's local storage (SharedPreferences). They are not tied to your account on the server, so they remain on the device even if you log out and back in. If you uninstall the app, your favorites will be cleared.

## Search

A **Search** bar sits at the top of both the **Doors** tab and the **Favorites** tab. As you type, the door list filters in real time to show only doors whose names match your query. Clear the search field to return to the full list.

Search is case-insensitive — typing "suite" will match "Suite 604", "Suite 701", and so on.

You can combine search with the floor filter chips on the **Doors** tab to quickly zero in on a specific door on a specific floor.
